# Mukti Marketing Plan

Date: 2026-03-17
Owner: Marketing Manager
Source issue: MUK-9

## Product Readout

Mukti is not a generic AI assistant. It is a Socratic thinking workspace positioned around "Liberation from AI Dependency." The public site currently sells the philosophy and captures waitlist demand. The private product already contains enough substance for a focused early-access push: waitlist-gated signup, authenticated Socratic chat, a structured Thinking Canvas, free-form Thought Maps, and conversation-to-map extraction.

This means the immediate go-to-market job is not broad awareness. It is message tightening, qualified early-user acquisition, and a private beta loop that proves the product creates better thinking outcomes than answer-first AI tools.

## Positioning

### Core positioning

Mukti helps people use AI without outsourcing their thinking.

### Recommended category language

Use: "Socratic thinking workspace" or "thinking layer for AI users."

Avoid leading with: "anti-AI," "productivity app," or broad mental wellness framing. Those either narrow the audience the wrong way or blur the product's differentiator.

### Primary wedge

Start with developers and technical learners who already use ChatGPT, Claude, or Cursor, but do not want shallow understanding, blind copy-paste, or reduced problem-solving ability.

Why this wedge:

- The product examples and RFCs are already strongest in technical problem-solving.
- Developers are unusually aware of the tradeoff between speed and understanding.
- They are easy to reach through founder-led content, communities, and waitlist interviews.

### Secondary audiences after the first wedge works

- Students doing serious independent study
- Founders and operators working through messy decisions
- Researchers and writers who need structured reasoning, not generated prose

## Messaging Framework

### Main promise

Think better with AI, not less.

### Supporting messages

- Mukti asks better questions instead of handing you shallow answers.
- It turns vague problems into visible reasoning paths.
- It helps you keep ownership of insight, judgment, and next steps.
- It is built for people who want cognitive leverage without cognitive atrophy.

### Proof points already visible in the product

- Socratic chat instead of answer-first prompting
- Structured canvas for assumptions, constraints, and insights
- Thought Maps for branching reasoning
- Conversation-to-map extraction for turning dialogue into reusable structure
- Waitlist-gated access that supports a controlled private beta

## 90-Day Go-To-Market Plan

### Phase 1: Clarify the story and instrument the funnel (Week 1-2)

- Tighten homepage copy from poetic/philosophical toward problem + mechanism + outcome.
- Add a clearer "who this is for" section for developers and technical learners.
- Keep the research-backed angle, but use it to support the product story rather than dominate it.
- Track baseline funnel metrics: landing visits, waitlist joins, signup activation, first conversation, first map/canvas creation, return rate.
- Define the onboarding path for approved waitlist users so marketing can measure activation, not just signups.

### Phase 2: Run founder-led demand discovery (Week 2-4)

- Interview 15-20 waitlist users or relevant prospects from the developer/technical learner segment.
- Publish short founder-led posts around the core pain:
  - "AI makes me faster but less sure I understand the work."
  - "Why answer-first AI creates shallow confidence."
  - "What a Socratic interface changes in practice."
- Share real product workflows: debugging via guided inquiry, problem decomposition, conversation-to-map thinking.
- Capture language from interviews and reuse it in landing copy, onboarding, and waitlist emails.

### Phase 3: Private beta launch (Week 4-8)

- Admit a small cohort of qualified waitlist users in batches.
- Send a structured onboarding email sequence:
  - why Mukti exists
  - how to use chat vs canvas vs thought maps
  - one suggested first workflow
  - request for feedback after first meaningful session
- Offer 20-minute onboarding calls for high-signal users.
- Collect 5-10 detailed case studies focused on "before Mukti / after Mukti" thinking quality.

### Phase 4: Repeatable growth experiments (Week 8-12)

- Test three content tracks:
  - anti-shallow-thinking thought leadership
  - product walkthroughs
  - user stories / case studies
- Test three acquisition routes:
  - founder social distribution
  - developer communities
  - direct outreach to waitlist users who match the ICP
- Build a lightweight referral ask into successful beta onboarding once retention is visible.

## Funnel Design

### Top of funnel

- Homepage
- Founder posts and essays
- Product walkthrough clips and screenshots
- Community posts where the pain is already understood

### Middle of funnel

- Waitlist signup
- Welcome email that explains who Mukti is for
- Qualification form or reply prompt to identify high-intent users

### Bottom of funnel

- Private beta access
- First-session activation
- Follow-up interview or feedback request
- Case study capture and referral ask

## Initial Growth Experiments

1. Rewrite the hero around the practical promise:
   "Use AI without losing the habit of thinking for yourself."

2. Create one flagship demo:
   a developer debugging workflow that starts in chat, branches in a thought map, and ends with a clearer self-authored conclusion.

3. Build a 5-email waitlist sequence:
   philosophy, product demo, research rationale, early-access invitation, feedback request.

4. Run message testing with two landing variants:
   one led by "AI dependency" and one led by "better reasoning with AI."

5. Ask every activated user one qualifying question:
   "What kind of work are you afraid AI is making you worse at?"

## Metrics That Matter

Focus on learning and activation first, not vanity reach.

- Landing page to waitlist conversion
- Waitlist to approved-user conversion
- Approved user to first meaningful session
- First session to second-week retention
- Share of users who create a map or canvas after chat
- Number of strong qualitative testimonials and case studies

## Dependencies and Risks

### Dependencies

- Clear approval flow from waitlist to product access
- Product analytics on activation and retention events
- Stable onboarding path for new users
- Fast feedback loop between product, design, engineering, and marketing

### Risks

- The current site is strong on philosophy but still light on practical use-case clarity.
- "Liberation from AI dependency" is memorable, but it can read as anti-AI unless paired with concrete product outcomes.
- Too broad an initial audience will dilute message quality and feedback quality.
- If beta onboarding is slow or confusing, marketing will over-measure top-of-funnel and under-learn on retention.

## Recommended Immediate Actions

1. Rewrite the landing page to balance philosophy with concrete use cases and visible workflows.
2. Define the primary beta ICP as developers and technical learners.
3. Add event tracking for waitlist join, signup, first chat, first canvas, first map, and week-two return.
4. Prepare a founder-led content calendar with 8-10 posts tied to real product behavior.
5. Start a private beta interview loop before spending effort on broad distribution.

## Bottom Line

Mukti should launch as a focused early-access product for people who want AI leverage without surrendering their reasoning. The fastest path is a narrow ICP, concrete product storytelling, tight onboarding, and rapid learning from a small cohort instead of a broad awareness push.
